Tabelog is uniquely dominant. Professional critics matter less in Japan than the Tabelog community score. The Tabelog Award (annual) is the most watched restaurant recognition event domestically.

### GEORGIA

#### Note on Georgia
Georgia lacks a formal restaurant guide system or established food critic tradition in the professional sense. Coverage comes primarily from:
- **International food writers** who have relocated (Slow Travel News author based in Tbilisi since 2020)
- **Wander-Lush** (wander-lush.org) -- most comprehensive Tbilisi restaurant guide (50+ restaurants, updated annually)
- **Chef-driven reputation**: Tekuna Gachechiladze (Cafe Littera) is the most prominent culinary figure
- Georgian wine and food are better covered by international publications than domestic criticism

## KEY PATTERNS & NOTES

**Dominant local platforms that eclipse professional critics:**
- Japan: Tabelog
- South Korea: Blue Ribbon Survey + Mangoplate
- Thailand: Wongnai
- Hong Kong: OpenRice
- Singapore: Makansutra (for hawker/street food)

**Countries where Gault&Millau matters more than Michelin:**
- Austria (Michelin barely covers it)
- Switzerland
- Germany (comparable)
- Belgium (comparable)
- Netherlands (comparable)

**Countries with strong domestic guides rivaling Michelin:**
- Spain: Guia Repsol (808 restaurants with Soles)
- Italy: Gambero Rosso + Slow Food Osterie d'Italia (outsells Michelin)
- Czech Republic: Maurer's Grand Restaurant
- South Korea: Blue Ribbon Survey
- France: Le Fooding (for bistronomie/casual)
- Australia: Good Food Guide (hat system)
- Portugal: Boa Cama Boa Mesa

**Countries with thin domestic critical infrastructure (rely on international sources):**
- Colombia, Georgia, Serbia (growing but nascent)
- Peru (Summum is the sole major domestic authority)

**Recent major changes (2024-2026):**
- Jay Rayner moved from Observer to Financial Times (UK)
- Pete Wells retired from NYT; replaced by Tejal Rao + Ligaya Mishan (USA)
- Gault&Millau returned to Poland after 5-year absence
- Michelin entered Argentina (2024), expanded in Turkey (Cappadocia), Greece (Thessaloniki, Santorini)
- Noma closed (Copenhagen, 2024)
- Vittles launched print edition (UK, 2025)
- Ignacio Medina published final column after 43 years (Spain/Peru)
